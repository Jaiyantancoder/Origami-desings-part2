CAMEL

  1. Camels have 3 layers of eyelids to protect thier eyes from sandstroms
  2. Camels can completly close thier nostrils during a sandstorm
  3. When a camel finally does find water, he can drink up to 40 gallons in one go

Chameleon

  1. Chameleons are reptiles that are part of iguana suborder
  2. Changing skin colors is an important form of communication among the chameleons
  3. Most chameleon have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches

Cicada

  1. Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs
  2. The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus
  3. They’re true bugs (from the order Hemiptera). So you can call them a bug and be scientifically accurate

Teddy Bear

  1. Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  2. The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.
  3. The world's smallest stitched teddy bear is a mere 0.29 inches tall! It was created by Cheryl Moss.

Pigeon

  1. Pigeons are known for thier incredible navigational abilities
  2. Pigeons are incredibly smart and intelligent birds
  3. Pigeons are highly social animals and can be seen flocks of 20-30 birds.

Panda

  1. Pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim
  2. An adult "Panda" can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!
  3. Prehistoric pandas lived up to "2 MILLION" years ago!!!!
